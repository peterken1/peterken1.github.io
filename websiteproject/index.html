Don't Be Hacked!

The internet and our going online to search or buy items has become a normal way of our day to day life. This has now become almost a basic necessity to the extent that we mostly approach our interactions without a good level of caution.

Most web applications require us to fill in and submit some level of our personal Information for us to use them. Many businesses with an online presence also want our personal data so that they can communicate with us through emails or for us to log in to their platforms. This is where the danger arises. Most people use very weak passwords or those that are easy to crack.

From the above image, you can see how easy or hard it is for someone with malicious intent to get hold of your password and end up eventually having your personal information which they can then use to scam you or worse still to even impersonate you and commit crime.

It is very important not to be too trusting online. Treat almost all interactions online with with a decent level of suspicion especially if it involves your parting with sensitive information like credit card numbers and passwords. see more advice below!

  • 1. Make sure that you use complex passwords with a mixture of characters and numbers. Easy passwords are a no no!. Don't use names that someone can collect through your social media profile.
  • 2. Before you sign up for any service online, ask yourself if you are signing up for an essential service and if you can not do without. Avoid the developing a habit of signing up for everything
  • 3. If you are about to release your personal information in order to get access to something online, it is important to do due diligence first just to find out if the company is reputable.
  • 4. Read the terms of service and how your information will be stored and used. Check if you can unsubscribe from the service at any time without complications.
"Always remember that if an offer sounds too good to be true, in most cases it is a scam!".
